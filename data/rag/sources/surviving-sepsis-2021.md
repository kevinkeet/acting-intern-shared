The Surviving Sepsis Campaign 2021 international guideline frames early sepsis management around a time-critical bundle. The core early actions are: measure lactate (and remeasure if elevated above 2 mmol/L), obtain blood cultures before antibiotics, give broad-spectrum antibiotics, begin 30 mL/kg of crystalloid for hypotension or lactate of 4 mmol/L or higher, and start vasopressors (norepinephrine first line) to maintain a mean arterial pressure of at least 65 mm Hg if the patient remains hypotensive.

A key 2021 nuance is risk-stratified urgency for antibiotics: when shock is present or the likelihood of sepsis is high, give antimicrobials immediately, ideally within one hour; when sepsis is possible but not yet confirmed and there is no shock, a brief period of rapid investigation (up to a few hours) is acceptable to avoid reflexive broad-spectrum overuse.

Teaching points: blood cultures BEFORE antibiotics (but never delay antibiotics in shock to obtain them), lactate as both a resuscitation target and a marker of perfusion, and the deliberate tension between "treat fast" and "do not over-treat every possible infection." The hour-1 bundle is influential but its strict timing remains debated.
